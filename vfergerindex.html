    What a client can expect?


    This blog mock-up was created for Brittany Ferries who are the leading maritime carrier on the western and central UK Channel. The purpose of the blog was to connect employees from France, The UK and Spain. The site is used to share images from holidays and make people more connected. It is a private social media and blog site within the company. The client was not sure if they actually need it so they asked to design:

    • Blog home page
    • Profile page where for ‘Gems’ (posts)
    • Portfolio page (archive)



    Users point of view


    The objective was to combine blog features with social media features and simplify it much as possible. I am not talking about multifunctional social media sites like Facebook. Brittany Ferries blog must connect employers and allow them to share the information they want. What the user will get out of this website is:

    • User friendly interaction
    • Easy navigation
    • Ability to post, share, save, store
    • Connect with other employees
    • Explore new places
    • Research new places
    • Company announcements

    Homelessness Situation


    The government’s proposed plan to end rough sleeping by 2027 is still on its way. However, before that happens homelessness is still a big issue in our society. Thus, the current situation inspired me to create a food support campaign for the people who live on the streets. Only by sharing and contributing can we achieve social justice for vulnerable people in our society.

    Solution


    Chip in is a food token campaign which will encompass local food establishments, the general public sector and homeless people.

    The tokens will be handed into local food establishments, sold to the general public and tokens will be spread by the public to the people who need them the most (mainly homeless people).

    Also, this campaign is different because one token is suitable for all food establishment who want to be part of the campaign and raise awareness of homelessness issue.

    Research


    To reach a credible solution I have carried out these research methods:

    • Interviews
    • Phone interviews
    • public talks
    • Emails


    One of the most important pieces of information I have gathered was from having interviews with homeless people. They revealed the true situation and struggle they are facing every day.

    Web Development


    As we move further into the digital age I have designed a campaign website as a charitable organisations need to grow their online presence in order to survive is becoming more and more apparent. Moreover, it is crucial to have a website for raising awareness, encouraging donations, and maintaining relationships with the supporters to keep the all-important funds flowing.




    Objectives


    The main objective is to incorporate brand a identity with a minimalistic layout to keep it consistent so the audience will recognise it and navigate intuitively without effort:

    • Basic awareness, promotions, mission, values, vision.
    • Encouraging donation sign ups
    • What we do
    • How it works
    • Statistics
    • Token purchase system

    When I designed this website I thought about “Hick’s Law“ which states that the time it takes to make a decision increases with the number and complexity of choices ,hence this website is simplified much as possible without cognitive overload. A user can just simply follow the steps implemented into website and achieve their desired goals without spending time learning how to use website.
